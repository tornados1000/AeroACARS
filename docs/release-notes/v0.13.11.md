### Technische Details (für die Neugierigen)

- **Trigger:** vorheriger Snapshot airborne in {Climb/Cruise/Holding/Descent/Approach/Final} UND neuer Snapshot `on_ground=true` UND |Altitude-Δ| > 5000 ft in einem Sample-Intervall

- **Distance bewusst NICHT geprüft:** XP12-Crash-Recovery spawnt 10-20 km vom Cruise-Punkt (unter 50-nm-Drift-Schwelle), wäre verpasst worden
- **Sim-agnostisch:** funktioniert für MSFS und XP12, da im zentralen Streamer-Loop implementiert (nicht im Adapter)

## 🇬🇧 English

**v0.13.11 — Mid-flight sim crash detection. When XP12 or MSFS crashes mid-flight and respawns you at the nearest airport, AeroACARS now stops cleanly, asks what to do, and does NOT write phantom ground touches into your PIREP.**

You jump directly from v0.13.8 to v0.13.11 — intermediate releases v0.13.9 and v0.13.10 were deliberately not rolled out to pilots because internal QA found more improvements. All of those are included in v0.13.11.

### What you get

**Real-world finding from GSG today:** Pilot Michel had **3 XP12 sim crashes** on a 7h transatlantic flight. Each time he was respawned from FL320-360 to the nearest airport on the ground (EDTD Donaueschingen, LFRK Caen, CYYT St. John's). AeroACARS streamed all three through silently — the PIREP route now shows three phantom ground touches.

From v0.13.11, AeroACARS detects such sim reloads **automatically**:

1. **Detection:** Were you in cruise, and suddenly a 5000+ ft altitude jump + on_ground=true comes through → clearly a sim reload (physically impossible otherwise)
2. **Recording paused immediately** — no more dubious snapshots go to phpVMS or VPS
3. **Heartbeat keeps running** — phpVMS keeps your PIREP alive even if you leave the banner open for hours (break, phone, etc.)
4. **ResumeFlightBanner appears** with three options:
   - **"Check position + resume"** — slew back to cruise position, click re-check, if drift < 5 nm: flight continues normally, no marking
   - **"Continue anyway"** — accept the break, jump logged in activity log, flight continues
   - **"Discard flight"** — full abort
5. **Multiple crashes in the same flight** trigger the banner cleanly each time

### Technical details (for the curious)

- **Trigger:** previous snapshot airborne in {Climb/Cruise/Holding/Descent/Approach/Final} AND new snapshot `on_ground=true` AND |altitude delta| > 5000 ft in one sample interval
- **Conservative:** max real descent ~4000 fpm = ~2000 ft per 30-s tick. 5000-ft drop in one tick is physically impossible = clearly reload
- **Distance check deliberately omitted:** XP12 crash recovery spawns 10-20 km from cruise point (below 50-nm drift threshold), would have been missed
- **Sim-agnostic:** works for both MSFS and XP12, implemented in the central streamer loop (not per-adapter)
- **Tests:** 8 new unit tests with real values from Michel's 3 reloads (all detected) + 5 false-positive scenarios (normal descent, normal landing, BlocksOn phase, etc., correctly rejected)

### QA findings fixed before pilot rollout

Internal QA found 4 more points before going to pilots:

1. **Heartbeat gap during banner** — phpVMS would have killed the PIREP after 2h banner time. Now: heartbeat keeps running with the last good pre-recovery snapshot
2. **Re-trigger on multi-crash** — banner only showed on first crash. Now: every subsequent crash triggers anew (relevant for repeat-crashers like Michel)
3. **`last_good_snap` reset** — heartbeat body was being built with ground/reload snapshot instead of real pre-recovery snapshot. Now corrected
4. **UI and activity-log text softened** — texts promised "PIREP will be marked untrusted" which we don't actually do automatically. New: "jump logged in activity log" (honest)
5. **npm dependency `ws`** — dev dependency updated to 8.21.0 (no impact on installed app, was only in Vitest toolchain)
6. **Cargo.lock** — internal crate versions aligned to 0.13.11
